# Source Evaluation and Reliability Assessment

Evaluating the reliability of sources is critical for providing accurate information.

## Conflict Resolution

When encountering conflicting information from different sources:

1. **Source Hierarchy Approach**:
   - Prioritize peer-reviewed sources over opinion pieces
   - Favor official sources over unofficial ones
   - Prefer recent sources over older ones when timeliness matters

2. **Cross-Verification**:
   - Look for additional sources that support each side
   - Check if differences are due to methodology or perspective
   - Identify consensus among authoritative sources

3. **Transparent Reporting**:
   - Acknowledge the existence of conflicting information
   - Explain possible reasons for discrepancies
   - Indicate which source is preferred and why
   - Suggest areas needing further research

## Reliability Indicators

### High Reliability Sources
- Peer-reviewed academic journals
- Government publications (.gov domains)
- Established educational institutions (.edu domains)
- Major news organizations with editorial standards
- Official documentation from technology companies
- Scientific organizations and institutions

### Medium Reliability Sources
- Established encyclopedias (Wikipedia with verification)
- Industry publications and trade magazines
- Professional organization websites
- Well-established blogs by recognized experts
- Technical forums with moderation (Stack Overflow, GitHub)

### Low Reliability Sources
- Personal blogs without author credentials
- Social media posts
- Forums without moderation
- Commercial sites primarily selling products
- Sites with clear ideological bias
- Anonymous sources

## Evaluation Criteria

### Author Credentials
- Check author's qualifications and expertise
- Look for institutional affiliations
- Verify professional standing in the field
- Consider potential conflicts of interest

### Publication Quality
- Editorial oversight presence
- Citation and reference practices
- Fact-checking procedures
- Correction and update policies

### Content Analysis
- Logical consistency of arguments
- Use of evidence and examples
- Balanced presentation of multiple viewpoints
- Absence of logical fallacies
- Proper distinction between facts and opinions

### Recency and Relevance
- Publication or last update date
- Continued relevance of the information
- Superseding research or developments
- Timeliness for the specific query

## Verification Techniques

### Cross-Referencing
- Check facts against multiple independent sources
- Look for consensus among reliable sources
- Note discrepancies and investigate causes
- Prioritize primary sources over secondary reporting

### Fact-Checking Resources
- Snopes, PolitiFact, FactCheck.org for claims
- Official statistics bureaus for data
- Scientific databases for research findings
- Official company websites for corporate information

### Domain and URL Analysis
- Examine the domain extension and organization
- Check WHOIS information for registration details
- Look for suspicious URL structures
- Verify SSL certificates for secure sites

## Warning Signs

Be cautious of sources exhibiting these characteristics:

1. **Sensational Headlines**: Clickbait or emotionally charged language
2. **Lack of Author Information**: No identifiable author or organization
3. **No Publication Date**: Unclear when information was published or updated
4. **Poor Writing Quality**: Grammatical errors, spelling mistakes
5. **Unsupported Claims**: Assertions without evidence or citations
6. **Bias Indicators**: One-sided presentation, loaded language
7. **Commercial Motivation**: Heavy advertising, affiliate links
8. **Conspiracy Mentality**: Claims of secret knowledge or cover-ups
9. **Circular Reporting**: Stories that all cite the same original source
10. **Outdated Information**: Clearly stale information presented as current

## Documentation Standards

When citing sources:

1. **Provide Attribution**: Clearly indicate source of information
2. **Include Publication Dates**: When available, include dates
3. **Link to Original Sources**: When possible, provide direct links
4. **Quote Precisely**: Use quotation marks for direct quotes
5. **Summarize Accurately**: Ensure summaries represent original content fairly
6. **Note Limitations**: Acknowledge uncertainties or limitations in sources
